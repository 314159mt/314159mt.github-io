National Employment Standards (NES)

The National Employment Standards (NES) are a set of minimum employment entitlements that apply to all employees in Australia. These standards are outlined in the Fair Work Act 2009 and provide a safety net of rights and obligations in the workplace.

The minimum entitlements of the National Employment Standards are:

Maximum weekly hours

The NES establishes maximum weekly hours for employees. An employer must not request or require an employee to work more than 38 hours per week, unless the additional hours are reasonable.

Requests for flexible working arrangements

Certain employees have the right to request flexible working arrangements regarding their work location, pattern, and hours. These employees include:

  • Parents or carers of a child who is of school age or younger
  • Carers
  • People with a disability
  • People aged 55 or older
  • Pregnant employees
  • Employees experiencing family and domestic violence, or supporting a family member experiencing family and domestic violence

Casual employment

A person is considered a casual employee if, at the commencement of their employment, there is no firm advance commitment to ongoing work, considering various factors. Casual employees are typically entitled to a casual loading or a specific casual pay rate as per an award, registered agreement, or employment contract.
